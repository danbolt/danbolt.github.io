About

Hello! My name's Daniel, and I'm 23 years old! I currently live in the all-too-perfect city of Victoria, British Columbia, and recently graduated from the University of Victoria with a Bachelor's of Computer Science.

My interests include video games, programming languages, hiking, and cooking. I particularily enjoy drinking coffee on Saturday afternoons with my friends. My favourite book is The Brothers Karamazov by Fyodor Dostoyevsky.

Someday, I hope to have developed a bunch of fun video games, become a versatile cook, and maybe even finish something published by ATLUS. The third one is a bit of a long-shot though.

Projects

Contract: Void Justice

Contract: Void Justice is a science fiction action game with procedurally-generated levels. The game involves the player trying to survive a prison break on a large spaceship. Throughout the course of the game, the player's decisions in which enemies they fight determines later gameplay, as well as the final ending. It can also be played co-operatively with a friend.

Zippy Push Kid

Zippy Push Kid is a platformer, starring Zippy the magnet, showcasing five frantic levels. Zippy cannot jump in the game, and instead must use magnetic powers to maneuver. Using the right analog stick, the player can propel Zippy, push objects, and even defeat enemies. Players can replay levels to attempt to acheive a record time.

Prince Protect

Prince Protect is a “boyfriend-defense” arcade game. One to two players attempt to keep their princes safe from ninjas as long as they can. Alongside physical violence, but players can strategically place colored barriers around the play area. If four barriers are placed together, they’ll transform into an item that the player(s) can use to their advantage. The concepts for the gameplay mechanics were borrowed from The Legend of Zelda and Puyo Puyo.

GitHub Download

Tumblr. The Game.

Tumblr. The Game. is a trivia challenge where you play as an alien attempting to study humanity through Tumblr. Players are presented a series of images pulled via Tumblr's API, and must guess the hashtag that connects them all together. This was the brainchild of my good friend Rita, and I helped her with the design and implementation.
